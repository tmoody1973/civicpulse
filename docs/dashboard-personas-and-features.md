## Persona 1: The Engaged Citizen

### Profile

**Name:** Sarah Martinez
**Age:** 34
**Occupation:** High school teacher
**Location:** Milwaukee, WI
**Political engagement:** Votes in every election, attends town halls occasionally
**Time available:** 15-20 minutes per day for civic engagement
**Tech savvy:** Moderate - uses iPhone, social media, but not deeply technical

### Current Pain Points

- "I want to stay informed, but reading 40-page bills is impossible with my schedule"
- "I don't know if my representatives are actually doing anything or just tweeting"
- "By the time I hear about important bills, the vote already happened"
- "I care about education and healthcare, but Congress covers everything"
- "I want to contact my reps, but I don't know WHAT to say about specific bills"

### How Sarah Would Use the Dashboard

#### **Morning Routine (5 minutes)**

1. **Open dashboard on phone while drinking coffee**
   - Glance at "Latest Actions" widget to see what happened yesterday
   - Check if any bills related to education (her interest) have new updates
   - See if her representatives (Tammy Baldwin, Gwen Moore) took any actions

2. **Listen to daily brief during commute (7 minutes)**
   - Audio podcast summarizing 3 most important bills from yesterday
   - Conversational hosts explain complex legislation in plain English
   - Includes how HER representatives voted or if they're sponsors

#### **Weekly Deep Dive (15 minutes - Sunday evening)**

1. **Review "My Representatives" widget**
   - See voting record visualization: How often do they vote with their party?
   - Check bills sponsored/co-sponsored this month
   - Read recent tweets or press releases (if available)

2. **Explore bills by interest**
   - Filter by "Education" and "Healthcare" tags
   - See impact scores: "High impact - affects teacher pay nationwide"
   - Read AI-generated summaries in 2-3 sentences

3. **Take action**
   - Click "Contact Rep" button with pre-filled message about specific bill
   - Share important bills on social media directly from dashboard
   - Set alerts for bills moving to committee votes

### Features That Would Make Sarah Come Back Daily

#### **Must-Have Features**

1. **Personalized Daily Audio Brief (5-7 minutes)**
   - Automatically generated based on her interests (education, healthcare)
   - Mentions HER representatives specifically
   - Available for offline listening
   - Push notification when new brief is ready (8am)

2. **Representative Performance Dashboard**
   - "Your Reps This Month" snapshot:

4. **Smart Notifications**
   - "üö® H.R. 1234 (Education Funding) moves to Senate vote tomorrow"
   - "üì¢ Rep. Moore just sponsored a bill you care about"
   - "‚è∞ Town hall with Sen. Baldwin in 3 days - [Add to calendar]"

   - "Contact Your Rep" button generates context-aware message:
     - "I'm a constituent in District 4"

#### **Nice-to-Have Features**

   - "Compare H.R. 1234 vs S. 567 - both address teacher pay"

## Persona 2: The Political Journalist

### Profile

**Name:** Marcus Thompson
**Age:** 29
**Occupation:** Congressional reporter for regional news outlet
**Location:** Washington, D.C.
**Beat:** Wisconsin congressional delegation
**Time available:** Checks dashboard 4-5 times per day
**Tech savvy:** High - uses multiple tools, APIs, data analysis

### Current Pain Points

- "I need to track 10 representatives' activities across hundreds of bills"
- "Congress.gov is slow and hard to search quickly"
- "By the time news breaks, I'm already behind other outlets"
- "I need to identify important bills BEFORE they blow up"
- "Connecting bills to constituent impact is time-consuming research"

### How Marcus Would Use the Dashboard

#### **Morning News Cycle (6am - 10am)**

1. **Check "Latest Actions" feed**
   - Scan all Wisconsin delegation activity overnight
   - Click into bill details for breaking news potential
   - Download full bill text with one click

2. **Identify Story Angles**
   - Filter by "High Impact" + "Wisconsin-relevant"
   - Check which bills have bipartisan co-sponsors (unusual, newsworthy)
   - See if delegation members are on opposite sides of controversial bills

3. **Research for Story**
   - Read AI summaries to understand complex legislation quickly
   - Check voting records: "How often do Baldwin and Johnson disagree?"
   - Export data to CSV for analysis in Excel/Google Sheets

#### **Afternoon Updates (1pm - 3pm)**

1. **Track Breaking Developments**
   - Real-time alerts: "üö® S. 1234 added to Senate floor calendar"
   - Committee hearing schedules: "Banking Committee vote tomorrow"
   - Amendment tracker: "3 new amendments filed to H.R. 567"

2. **Build Context for Articles**
   - Historical voting patterns of Wisconsin delegation
   - Bill co-sponsorship network: "Who works with whom?"
   - Impact analysis: "This bill affects 1.2M Wisconsin residents"

#### **Evening Filing (5pm - 7pm)**

1. **Write Story with Dashboard Data**
   - Copy bill summaries (with citation)
   - Screenshot representative voting record charts
   - Link directly to bills for readers

2. **Plan Tomorrow's Coverage**
   - Set alerts for specific bills moving to votes
   - Mark representatives to follow closely
   - Create custom dashboard view: "Week Ahead Preview"

### Features That Would Make Marcus Come Back Hourly

#### **Must-Have Features**

1. **Advanced Search & Filtering**
   - Boolean search: `"climate" AND (sponsor:Baldwin OR cosponsor:Moore)`
   - Filter by:
     - Congressional committee
     - Bill status (introduced, committee, floor, passed)
     - Policy area + state impact
     - Bipartisan (co-sponsors from both parties)
     - Vote margin (close votes = newsworthy)

2. **Real-Time Activity Feed**
   - "‚ö° LIVE: Floor vote happening now on H.R. 1234"
   - "üìã New bill introduced: S. 5678 (Climate)"
   - "üó£Ô∏è Rep. Moore speaking on House floor [Live stream link]"
   - Push notifications for breaking developments

3. **Representative Comparison Dashboard**
   - Side-by-side voting records
   - "Baldwin vs Johnson: 73% voting divergence"
   - Issue-by-issue breakdown: Healthcare, Defense, Economy
   - Party loyalty scores
   - Who's the most bipartisan?

4. **Data Export & API Access**
   - Export to CSV, JSON, or Excel
   - Direct API endpoints for automated data pulls
   - Embeddable widgets for news articles
   - Historical data: "Show me all education bills from 2020-2025"

5. **Bill Tracking & Monitoring**
   - Create custom watch lists: "Wisconsin Dairy Industry Bills"
   - Get digest emails: "5 bills you're tracking had activity today"
   - Set complex alerts: "Alert me if ANY Wisconsin rep sponsors a gun bill"
   - Share watch lists with colleagues

6. **Legislative Intelligence**
   - **Prediction scores**: "70% chance of passing Senate"
   - **Lobbying data**: "Top 5 organizations supporting this bill"
   - **Amendment tracker**: See all proposed changes in real-time
   - **Committee vote schedules**: "Banking Committee markup session tomorrow"
   - **Historical context**: "Similar bill failed in 2022 by 3 votes"

#### **Nice-to-Have Features**

7. **Newsroom Collaboration Tools**
   - Share annotated bills with colleagues
   - Internal notes on bills: "Good quote from Baldwin's staff"
   - Team alerts: "Marcus is already covering H.R. 1234"

8. **Source Contact Management**
   - Link congressional staffers to representatives
   - Quick access to press contacts
   - Track communication: "Last contacted Baldwin's office on 11/3"

9. **Story Angle Suggestions**
   - "This bill affects manufacturing - Wisconsin angle"
   - "Only state with split delegation voting this way"
   - "Historical precedent: Similar to 2008 Farm Bill"

10. **Press Release & Statement Tracking**
    - Aggregate all Wisconsin delegation press releases
    - Search statements by bill or topic
    - Compare rhetoric vs voting record

11. **Visual Storytelling Tools**
    - Generate shareable charts/infographics
    - Voting record heatmaps
    - Bill progress timelines
    - Network graphs of co-sponsorship

### What Would Make Marcus Stop Using It

‚ùå Slow search (needs instant results)
‚ùå Data inaccuracies (even small errors destroy trust)
‚ùå No historical data (needs context from previous sessions)
‚ùå Can't export data (needs to analyze in own tools)

## Conclusion

The dashboard must serve **two distinct but complementary audiences**:

1. **Engaged citizens** need simplicity, context, and actionable tools to participate in democracy without spending hours researching.

2. **Political journalists** need speed, depth, and data access to report accurately on complex legislation under tight deadlines.

Success = Sarah uses it daily for 10 minutes AND Marcus uses it hourly as a professional tool.

**Next Steps:**
1. Validate personas with real users (5 interviews each)
2. Build MVP for engaged citizens first (larger addressable market)
3. Add professional journalist tools in Phase 3
4. Continuously measure engagement and iterate based on actual usage patterns
